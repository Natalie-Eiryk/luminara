# Narrative Adventure Mode: "The Waterfall Detour"

## Design Document v1.0

### Overview

A mad-libs style narrative roguelike where players are students sneaking off to the waterfall before class. Quiz questions are woven into story encounters, with wrong answers having narrative (and mechanical) consequences.

**Tone**: Playful mischief, not malicious. Think Ferris Bueller meets quiz game.

---

## Story Structure

### Act 1: Home (The Decision)
**Setting**: Your bedroom, morning alarm blaring

**Mad-Libs Elements**:
- Your excuse for being late: "I had to help my [NOUN] with their [ADJECTIVE] [NOUN]"
- What you pack in your bag: [ITEM], [ITEM], and definitely not your [SCHOOL SUPPLY]

**Encounters**:
1. **Mom's Suspicion Check** - Answer a question correctly or she gets suspicious (+1 suspicion meter)
2. **Finding Your Swimsuit** - Memory quiz (where did you leave it?)
3. **The Breakfast Dilemma** - Eat fast (time pressure) or skip (less HP for the day)

**Choice Point**:
- Go straight to school (BORING - skip to Act 4, but safe)
- "Take the scenic route" (THE ADVENTURE BEGINS)

---

### Act 2: The Path (Temptation Trail)
**Setting**: The woods between home and school

**Mad-Libs Elements**:
- What bird you see: "A [ADJECTIVE] [BIRD] that looks like it's judging you"
- Trail marker graffiti: "[NAME] was here, [YEAR]"

**Encounters**:
1. **The Fork in the Path** - Geography/nature question determines if you take a shortcut or get lost
2. **Suspicious Dog Walker** - Social encounter, answer correctly or they remember your face
3. **Your Phone Buzzes** - Friend asking where you are. Your typed excuse uses mad-libs
4. **The Warning Sign** - "DANGER: [MAD-LIB HAZARD]" - Reading comprehension

**Environmental Hazards**:
- Mud puddles (wrong answer = muddy clothes, +1 suspicion later)
- Low branches (wrong answer = messy hair)
- Poison ivy (wrong answer = itchy distraction, -1 to future rolls)

---

### Act 3: The Waterfall (Forbidden Paradise)
**Setting**: A beautiful hidden waterfall, crystal clear pool

**Mad-Libs Elements**:
- What you yell as you jump: "[EXCLAMATION]!"
- The name carved in the rock: "[NAME] + [NAME] 4EVR"
- What's floating in the water: "Is that a [NOUN]?"

**Encounters**:
1. **The Climb** - Physics question (force, friction) or you slip
2. **The Jump** - Courage check + biology question (what happens to your body in cold water?)
3. **The Splash Contest** - Math question (calculate splash radius)
4. **The Discovery** - You find something in the water: [MYSTERY ITEM]
5. **Time Check** - "What time is it?!" - Clock reading / time math

**Risk/Reward**:
- Staying longer = more fun items/buffs but higher suspicion
- Each extra swim = +1 to a stat but +1 minute late

**The Moment of Reckoning**:
- You hear the school bell in the distance
- Check the time: "CLASS STARTED [X] MINUTES AGO"

---

### Act 4: The Sneak (Infiltration)
**Setting**: School grounds, then hallways

**Mad-Libs Elements**:
- Your cover story: "I was at the [PLACE] doing [ACTIVITY]"
- The hall pass you forge: "Please excuse [NAME] from [CLASS] because [EXCUSE]"

**Encounters**:
1. **The Back Gate** - Locked! Combination is a math problem
2. **The Janitor** - Stealth check + question. Mr. [NAME] has seen everything.
3. **The Hall Monitor** - Your nemesis. Trivia battle.
4. **The Wet Footprints** - You're leaving a trail! Quick - biology question about evaporation
5. **Your Locker** - Combination forgotten? Memory/pattern question

**Suspicion System**:
- Accumulated suspicion from earlier acts affects difficulty
- High suspicion = more encounters, harder questions
- Teachers comment: "Why is your hair wet?" "Is that algae?"

---

### Act 5: Ms. Luminara (The Final Boss)
**Setting**: The classroom door. She's waiting.

**Her Opening Line** (mad-libs based on your run):
- "Ah, [PLAYER NAME]. How interesting that you smell like [WATERFALL ITEM] and your excuse involves [YOUR EXCUSE NOUN]."

**Boss Phases**:

**Phase 1: The Stare**
- She just... looks at you
- Intimidation resistance: answer questions to maintain composure
- Wrong answers: you start sweating, stammering

**Phase 2: The Questions**
- "Since you're SO eager to learn, let's see what you know"
- Rapid-fire quiz questions from your selected topics
- Each wrong answer: "Interesting. And you had time for the WATERFALL but not for STUDYING?"

**Phase 3: The Deduction**
- She pieces together your morning using clues
- "Your shoes are muddy - you took the forest path"
- "Your hair is wet - you went swimming"
- "You have a [MYSTERY ITEM] in your pocket"
- Player must answer questions to counter each deduction

**Phase 4: The Verdict**
- Based on performance, she delivers judgment
- But there's a twist...

**The Twist**:
- If you answered enough questions correctly throughout
- "Well, well. It seems the waterfall didn't wash away ALL your knowledge."
- "Perhaps... mischief and learning aren't mutually exclusive."
- She smiles (terrifying)
- "Detention. But bring your textbook. We're going to have... fun."

## Game Systems

### The Suspicion Meter
- Starts at 0
- Increases with wrong answers, bad choices, physical evidence
- Affects Act 4-5 difficulty
- Visual: Eye icon that opens wider as suspicion grows

### The Fun Meter
- Measures how much you enjoyed the detour
- High fun = better ending even if caught
- Low fun = why did you even bother?

### Mad-Libs Integration
- Player fills in blanks at key story moments
- Their choices appear in NPC dialogue later
- Creates personalized, replayable narrative
- Example: If they said their excuse involved "grandma" and "rocket ship", Ms. Luminara references it

### Item System
- **Towel**: Reduce wet evidence
- **Good Excuse**: One-time suspicion reducer
- **Snack**: Restore HP/focus
- **Mystery Item**: From waterfall, affects ending

### Time System
- Clock ticks during adventure
- More activities = more late = harder boss
- Speed-answering questions can save time

## Ms. Luminara Boss Dialogue Examples

**On seeing the player**:
- "Ah, my favorite [ADJECTIVE] student. How... damp you look today."
- "I was WONDERING when you'd grace us with your presence."
- "The waterfall called. It said you forgot your dignity there."

**On wrong answers**:
- "Fascinating. The waterfall has washed away your neurons."
- "Perhaps you should have studied instead of... what's that in your hair? Algae?"
- "Wrong. But don't worry, we have ALL of detention to review this."

**On correct answers**:
- "...Acceptable." (grudging respect)
- "So you CAN learn. Even while committing truancy."
- "Hmph. Lucky guess." (she knows it wasn't)

**Victory quotes**:
- "Well played, little fish. But I'll catch you next time."
- "Fine. You may sit. But I'm watching you. ALWAYS."
- "Perhaps there's hope for you yet. Slim hope. Microscopic hope."
