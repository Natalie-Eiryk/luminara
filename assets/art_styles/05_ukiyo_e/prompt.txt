A red fox on a hillside beneath cherry blossoms, Japanese ukiyo-e woodblock print style.
Fox rendered with bold black sumi ink outlines defining every form, filled with flat color
planes: burnt orange body (beni pigment), white chest and tail tip (gofun white), no Western
shading. Stylized fur indicated through black line work only, not tonal variation. Background
cherry tree with thick black trunk lines, blossoms as simplified pink circles with white centers,
branches arranged in decorative asymmetrical pattern. Rolling hills as flat color zones:
foreground emerald green, middle distance sage green, far distance pale blue-grey demonstrating
atmospheric perspective. Bokashi gradient in sky only: deep indigo at top transitioning to
pale horizon through traditional woodblock gradient technique. Stylized clouds as white curved
shapes with black outlines. Limited traditional pigment palette: beni red-orange, ai indigo
blue, green from indigo+yellow, pink from beni+white. Asymmetrical composition with fox in
lower left, cherry tree entering from upper right, diagonal movement across picture plane.
Negative space as active element. Decorative cartouche with mock kanji signature in corner.
Hokusai and Hiroshige influence, edo period aesthetic, woodblock texture with visible grain,
registration marks in margins.
