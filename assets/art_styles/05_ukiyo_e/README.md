## Overview
Ukiyo-e ("pictures of the floating world") dominated Japanese art through woodblock printing. This style profoundly influenced Western art through its flat color planes, bold outlines, and asymmetrical compositions.

**Subject**: Red fox on hillside beneath cherry blossoms
**File**: `exemplar.png` (MOCK - 23KB, real should be 1-2MB)

## Key Characteristics
- Bold black sumi ink outlines (variable width)
- Completely flat color fills (no Western shading)
- Limited traditional pigment palette (beni, ai, gofun)
- Asymmetrical composition with diagonal dynamics
- Stylized natural elements as graphic patterns
- Visible woodblock texture
- Active negative space (ma)
- Decorative cartouche with signature/seal

## Key Artists
Katsushika Hokusai, Utagawa Hiroshige, Kitagawa Utamaro, Tsukioka Yoshitoshi
