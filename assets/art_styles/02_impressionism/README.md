## Overview

Impressionism revolutionized art in the 1870s-1880s by prioritizing the artist's perception of light and color over photographic accuracy. Artists used visible brushstrokes, broken color theory, and emphasis on natural light to capture fleeting moments.

## Style Characteristics

**Visual Signature**:
- Visible energetic brushstrokes creating texture
- Broken color technique (pure pigments side-by-side)
- Soft atmospheric perspective (background dissolves into haze)
- Luminous high-key color palette
- No hard edges or precise detail
- Canvas texture visible (impasto highlights)

**Color Approach**:
- Optical mixing (violet shadows + orange sunlight)
- Pastel spring palette: soft pinks, pale greens, warm ochres, sky blues
- Cadmium yellow, cobalt blue, vermillion, viridian green
